Real-time Pixar Rendering

Reverse engineering Pixar's lighting techniques

A technical artist and his team were reverse engineering some of Pixar’s concept art, looking for what gave it the signature look. This shader is my attempt at getting a realtime lighting and shadowing system that would try to resemble Pixar’s illumination process. Uses half lambert with biases, ramps, light-driven saturation and a few more tricks. Works on mobile and webgl.

The video shows a thumbnail of how this model looks in games currently on the market. The big colorful version is our take on it.

Input Devices and Networking

Real-time control of dynamical systems

Using OSC/Midi to manage multiple devices and stream input into dynamic systems, for authoring, gaming and control. The video also shows a LAN multiplayer racing system we prototyped. With the Internet of Things approaching fast, P2P, IP6, WebRTC and wearable technology, the need for highly connected systems will be bigger than ever.

These types of interactions allow for real-time control of dynamical systems, including scrubbing, and exploration. And for improving iteration in creative developments.

Physics and Artificial Intelligence

When dynamical systems come to life

Bullet and custom Verlet Integration, Force-directed and PID controlled physics. Signal generators and signal-driven animations. One important aspect is how life-like behaviors emerge from independent control systems. PID controllers are one of my favorites tools.
